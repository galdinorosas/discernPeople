HARRY BLOUNT

CHAIRMAN + CEO DIRECTOR OF FORESIGHT

Investment research industry veteran Harry Blount formed the DISCERN founding team, building on more than 20 years of executive and financial experience on Wall Street, including senior roles at Lehman Brothers, Credit Suisse First Boston, Donaldson Lufkin & Jenrette, and CIBC Oppenheimer. Harry has been named an Institutional Investor All-American in both Information Technology Hardware and Internet Infrastructure Services, and The Wall Street Journal has recognized him as an All-Star covering the Computer Hardware sector.  Harry also lent his expertise to the National Academy of Sciences where he served as a member of the Committee for Forecasting Future Disruptive Technologies; and to The Tech Museum of Innovation in San Jose, where he chaired the Futures Committee. Harry graduated from the University of Wisconsin – La Crosse in 1986 with a B.S. in Finance.

CHIP DICKSON

Henry "Chip" Dickson, CFA Head of Curation Services

Before co-founding DISCERN, Chip was Chief Equity Strategist and Associate Director of Research at Lehman Brothers. Prior to that, Chip was one of Wall Street’s leading bank analysts. A nine-time Institutional Investor All-American analyst, he is also co-author of the book, The Great Deleveraging.

Chip was also a Bank Analyst at Salomon Smith Barney, Kemper Securities, and McDonald & Company. Before that he was a commercial banker in Cleveland. Chip is a Chartered Financial Analyst.

Chip serves on the Cutler Center Advisory Board at Babson College, where he earned a B.S.B.A. and an M.B.A., and was an Adjunct Professor at Seton Hall University.

MICHELE DUNDAS

Michele Dundas MacphersonChief Operating Officer

Chief Operating Officer, Michele Macpherson joined DISCERN in 2015 to accelerate the company’s growth. Previously, she was General Manager of NCR’s Entertainment Division (acquired by Coinstar). At NCR, she acquired The New Release and integrated the start-up into NCR’s existing infrastructure ensuring synergies were realized in sales, marketing, hosting/IT infrastructure, engineering, product management, technical support, customer service, manufacturing and field operations. She was a Senior Vice President of DVDPlay, acquired by NCR, which became part of the Entertainment Division where she drove development and deployment of self-serve kiosks in retail locations, built the consumer-acquisition and customer-value maximization program, creating a $30M business. Previously, she was SVP and CIO of identity management start-up ActivIdentity (acquired by Assa Abloy) and SVP of Encentuate (acquired by IBM). She worked with The Boston Consulting Group and CSC Index consulting to Fortune 100 companies on strategy, mergers and acquisitions and operations. Michele holds a Bachelor of Applied Science in Electrical Engineering from the University of Waterloo and an MBA from the Stanford Graduate School of Business. 

Kerner Smith

Kerner SmithChief Revenue Officer

Prior to joining DISCERN, Kerner Smith demonstrated his ample ability to identify and meet the needs of institutional fund managers over the course of ten years in equity sales at KeyBanc Capital Markets. At Key, Kerner was promoted rapidly, becoming the youngest Director in the firm’s history and a Managing Director in early 2010. His client relationship and servicing skills earned him recognition at KeyBanc as the salesperson of the year for 2009. Kerner earned a B.S. in Applied Economics, Management and Marketing at Cornell University. 

Vasu Gourabathina

Vasu GourabathinaSenior VP, Engineering

Vasu joined DISCERN following nearly two decades of success in architecting, designing, building, and deploying products and business solutions. Prior to joining DISCERN, Vasu held a variety of senior roles, including Senior Director of Augmentation Services at Augmentum, where he managed global engineering teams in the development and delivery of multiple products and solutions. Prior to that, Vasu held Tech Lead and Senior Engineer roles at Vitria Technology, SAP Labs, Oracle, Formtek, Inc., and Intergraph. He has built deep domain expertise in SaaS, EAI, ERP, BI and business process modeling in manufacturing, high-tech, healthcare, and finance verticals. 

Valerie St. John

Valerie St. JohnVP, Marketing

Valerie St. John is an award-winning communicator with more than a decade of experience in commercial television journalism and technology marketing. Prior to running DISCERN’s Marketing team, Valerie collaborated with stakeholders at CISCO Enterprise, driving video content for BizWise TV and TechWise TV to nurture prospective customers for Conferencing and Unified Communications product lines. Valerie is more widely known as a former broadcaster for KRON-TV (San Francisco), KING-TV (Seattle), and KTVO (Cedar Rapids), where she covered presidential politics. She began her career at National Public Radio (NPR) affiliate WAMU and remains a listener. She is a graduate of Northwestern University.
